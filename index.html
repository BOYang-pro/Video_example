Breaking Synthetic Dependency: Towards Real-world Unsupervised Multi-modal Image Registration and Fusion

Bo Yang1,2, Zhaohui Jiang1,*, Dong Pan1, Zhiping Lin2, Weihua Gui1
1 School of Automation, Central South University
2 Nanyang Technological University
*Corresponding author

Abstract

Multimodal image fusion is highly sensitive to spatial misalignment, which often leads to visual artifacts and structural distortions. In real-world scenarios, obtaining multimodal image pairs with ground-truth correspondence is a challenge. Most of the existing joint registration and fusion methods typically rely on synthetic data to mitigate the lack of ground truth. However, models trained on synthetic data often fail to generalize to real-world scenes with significant misalignment. To address this issue, this work first introduces an unsupervised multimodal image alignment method, termed bidirectional cycle-consistency alignment, which is designed to learn spatial correspondences directly from unaligned real data. Specifically, the method constructs an image triplet and introduces a flow-consistency principle to establish forward and backward constraints, achieving feature-level alignment. Building upon this, a fusion framework is designed that consists of a feature registration network and a feature fusion network. The former learns cross-modal spatial correspondences via an unsupervised alignment method. At the same time, the latter integrates multimodal features using a hybrid CNN-Mamba module to suppress redundant information and produce high-quality fusion results. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the proposed method achieves superior qualitative and quantitative performance on both public datasets and industrial scenarios. Moreover, applications in object detection and video processing further validate the effectiveness of the proposed method in downstream tasks.
